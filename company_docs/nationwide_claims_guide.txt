NATIONWIDE INSURANCE CLAIMS PROCESS GUIDE

OVERVIEW

At Nationwide, we understand that filing a claim can be stressful. Our goal is to make the process as smooth and efficient as possible, helping you get back to your normal life quickly. This guide outlines our claims process and what you can expect.

WHEN TO FILE A CLAIM

You should file a claim when:
- You've been in an auto accident (regardless of fault)
- Your property has been damaged by fire, theft, weather, or other covered perils
- You or others have been injured in an incident covered by your policy
- You need to make a liability claim

Important: File your claim as soon as possible after an incident, even if you don't have all the information. We can always add details later.

HOW TO FILE A CLAIM

24/7 CLAIM FILING OPTIONS:

1. ONLINE
   - Visit nationwide.com and log into your account
   - Click "File a Claim" under your policy
   - Complete the online form (takes 10-15 minutes)
   - Upload photos and documents

2. PHONE
   - Call 1-800-421-3535 (available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week)
   - Speak with a claims professional who will guide you through the process

3. MOBILE APP
   - Download the Nationwide Mobile app
   - Select "File a Claim"
   - Follow the step-by-step process
   - Take and upload photos directly from your phone

4. THROUGH YOUR AGENT
   - Contact your local Nationwide agent
   - They can help you start the claims process

INFORMATION YOU'LL NEED

AUTO CLAIMS:
- Policy number
- Date, time, and location of accident
- Description of how the accident occurred
- Contact and insurance information for other drivers involved
- Contact information for witnesses
- Police report number (if applicable)
- Photos of vehicle damage
- Photos of accident scene

PROPERTY CLAIMS:
- Policy number
- Date and time of loss
- Type of damage (fire, water, theft, wind, etc.)
- Description of damage
- List of damaged or stolen items
- Photos of damage
- Police report number (for theft or vandalism)
- Receipts for emergency repairs (if applicable)

THE CLAIMS PROCESS

STEP 1: FILE YOUR CLAIM
Submit your claim through any of the methods listed above. You'll receive a claim number immediately.

STEP 2: CLAIM ASSIGNMENT
Within 24 hours, you'll be assigned a dedicated claims associate who will:
- Contact you to discuss your claim
- Explain the next steps
- Answer any questions you have
- Schedule inspections if needed

STEP 3: INVESTIGATION
Your claims associate will:
- Review the details of your claim
- Determine coverage based on your policy
- Gather necessary information
- May inspect damage in person or request photos
- Obtain police reports or other documentation as needed

STEP 4: DAMAGE ASSESSMENT
For AUTO CLAIMS:
- Your vehicle will be inspected by a Nationwide appraiser or at an On Your Side Auto Repair Network shop
- We'll provide a repair estimate
- You can choose any repair shop, or select one from our network for guaranteed repairs

For PROPERTY CLAIMS:
- A claims associate or adjuster will inspect the damage
- We'll provide an estimate for repairs
- You can use your own contractor or select one from our On Your Side Property Repair Network

STEP 5: SETTLEMENT
Once the damage assessment is complete:
- We'll review the estimate with you
- Payment will be issued for covered damages minus your deductible
- For mortgaged properties, your mortgage company may be included on the payment check
- You can set up direct deposit for faster payment

STEP 6: REPAIRS
- Choose your repair vendor
- Repairs begin
- We stay in contact to ensure work progresses smoothly
- Network shops provide lifetime guarantees on their work

STEP 7: CLAIM CLOSURE
- Once repairs are complete and you're satisfied, we'll close your claim
- You'll receive final documentation
- Your claims associate will follow up to ensure everything is resolved

SPECIAL SITUATIONS

TOTAL LOSS (AUTO):
If your vehicle is totaled, we'll:
- Determine the actual cash value of your vehicle
- Pay you that amount minus your deductible
- Discuss settlement options with you
- Handle title transfer and salvage

UNLIVABLE HOME:
If your home is uninhabitable due to covered damage:
- We may cover additional living expenses (hotel, meals)
- This includes temporary housing while repairs are made
- Keep all receipts for reimbursement

RENTAL CAR:
If you have rental car reimbursement coverage:
- We'll help arrange a rental vehicle
- You choose the rental company
- We reimburse up to your policy limit
- Coverage lasts while your car is being repaired

THEFT:
If your vehicle or property is stolen:
- File a police report immediately
- Provide the police report number when filing your claim
- For auto theft, we typically wait 30 days before paying total loss (in case the vehicle is recovered)

TIPS FOR A SMOOTH CLAIMS PROCESS

BEFORE YOU FILE:
- Document everything with photos
- Keep all receipts related to the loss
- Don't make permanent repairs before inspection (unless necessary to prevent further damage)
- Cooperate with investigators
- Provide information promptly

AFTER YOU FILE:
- Respond to your claims associate quickly
- Keep records of all communications
- Save temporary repair receipts
- Don't cash settlement checks until you're satisfied with the amount
- Ask questions if anything is unclear

PREVENTING FURTHER DAMAGE

You're required to take reasonable steps to prevent additional damage:
- Tarp holes in your roof
- Board up broken windows
- Shut off water if pipes burst
- Move valuables to a safe, dry location
- Keep receipts for emergency repairs (we may reimburse these)

POLICY REQUIREMENTS

Remember:
- Report accidents to us even if you don't plan to file a claim
- Some policies require immediate notification
- Failure to report may affect coverage
- Never admit fault at the scene of an accident
- Cooperate with our investigation

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: How long does a claim take?
A: Simple claims may be resolved in a few days. Complex claims can take weeks or months depending on the extent of damage and investigation needed.

Q: Will my premium go up?
A: It depends on many factors including fault, severity, your claims history, and state regulations. Your agent can discuss this with you.

Q: Can I choose my own repair shop?
A: Yes, you can use any licensed repair facility. However, network shops offer benefits like lifetime guarantees.

Q: What if I disagree with the damage estimate?
A: Discuss your concerns with your claims associate. You can obtain your own estimate and we'll review it.

Q: Can I track my claim online?
A: Yes, use Claims Tracker in your online account or mobile app.
