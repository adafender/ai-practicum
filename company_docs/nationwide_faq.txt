CLAIMS

Q: How do I file a claim?
A: You can file a claim 24/7 by:
- Filing online at nationwide.com (takes 10-15 minutes)
- Calling 1-800-421-3535
- Using the Nationwide mobile app
- Contacting your local agent

Q: What information do I need to file a claim?
A: When filing a claim, have ready:
- Your policy number
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Description of what happened
- Contact information for anyone else involved
- Photos of damage (if applicable)
- Police report number (if applicable)

Q: How long does the claims process take?
A: Simple claims can often be settled within a few days. More complex claims may take longer depending on the extent of damage and investigation needed. You can check your claim status anytime online or by calling your claims associate.

Q: Will my rates go up if I file a claim?
A: It depends on several factors including your state, driving history, the type and severity of the claim, and who was at fault. In most states, if you're not at fault in an accident, your rates won't increase. We review each situation individually.

HOME INSURANCE

Q: What does homeowners insurance cover?
A: Homeowners insurance typically covers:
- Dwelling (structure of your home)
- Other structures (garage, shed, fence)
- Personal property (belongings)
- Liability protection
- Additional living expenses (if your home becomes uninhabitable)
Coverage is subject to policy limits, exclusions, and conditions.

Q: Do I need flood insurance?
A: Standard homeowners insurance does not cover flood damage. If you live in a flood-prone area, we strongly recommend purchasing separate flood insurance through the National Flood Insurance Program or a private insurer.

Q: How much coverage do I need?
A: Your home should be insured for its full replacement cost - the amount it would cost to rebuild your home from the ground up. Your personal property coverage should reflect the value of your belongings. We recommend conducting a home inventory to determine adequate coverage.
